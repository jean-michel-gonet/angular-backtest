What is an allocation strategy

This strategy consists in keeping a diversified portfollio keeping constant asset allocations. A regular rebalancing prevents allocations from drifting away with asset price variation.

Swiss CHF All Weather Portfolio

What about investing in Switzerland, with CHF based instruments?

The main reason to invest in your own domestic currency is to avoid being exposed to exchange rate risk. But, if you buy parts of a fund that is investing in a foreign economy, then you're exposed to exchage risk rate, regardless of the fund's currency.

For example, benchmark for Gold is XAU/USD, the price of gold in dollars. Therefore, variations of USD exchange rate are going to affect the price of any fund investing in gold, regardless if its base currency is USD, CHF or any other.

Same goes for commodities. Benchmark for commoditites is the BCOM, Bloomberg commodities index, which is expressed in USD. Commodities market is mostly in USD, therefore any fund investing in commodities is exposed to USD exchage rate variation.

So, why investing in funds that are based on CHF? There are two reasons. The first is simplicity, as you don't need to change CHF to USD, and then buy parts in USD. The second is because you may choose a fund investing in Switzerland:

SPICHA
This is the UBS fund based on the SPI, which represents more than 1500 capitalizations in the SIX stock exchange. This fund invests exclusively in Switzerland.
ZGLD
A fund from Zurich Kantonal Bank that invests in physical gold. Gold funds are of all kinds, most of them don't buy actual gold. This one does, and keeps it in a big safe in ZÃ¼rich. For 0.4% ter, it is a good fund.
DCCHAS
A fund by UBS investing in commodities. This is the one I would most probably leave to select some other with a lesser TER.
